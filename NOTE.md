# Gemma 4 Good Hackathon Project Direction

## Working Title

Classroom Access Kit

## Core Idea

An offline-first AI classroom and study kit that supports both teachers and students. It helps teachers create inclusive classroom material, and it also lets students who are blind, visually impaired, dyslexic, language-diverse, or struggling academically study the same material independently.

A teacher or student can take a photo of a textbook page, blackboard, worksheet, handwritten note, chart, diagram, or classroom object. Gemma 4 reads and understands the material locally, then turns it into useful teaching outputs and accessible study support.

## Main Users

- Teachers in low-connectivity schools
- Blind or visually impaired students who need audio-friendly and screen-reader-friendly study support
- Students who need accessible, simplified, or translated learning material
- Rural/community classrooms where privacy, cost, and internet access matter

## What It Does

Teacher Mode:
- Generate a lesson plan from a textbook page or blackboard photo
- Create quizzes, worksheets, activities, and homework
- Produce versions for beginner, average, and advanced students
- Translate or simplify the content for local-language learners

Accessible Reader Mode:
- Describe images, charts, diagrams, and handwritten classroom notes
- Convert textbook pages into clear spoken explanations
- Create screen-reader-friendly summaries
- Explain visual concepts step by step for blind or low-vision students
- Rewrite complex content for dyslexic or struggling readers

Student Study Mode:
- Let blind or visually impaired students study from classroom photos without depending fully on a sighted helper
- Turn textbook pages, board notes, worksheets, and diagrams into structured audio-friendly explanations
- Answer student questions about the material in simple language
- Break lessons into step-by-step study notes, key points, and practice questions
- Help students revise independently using voice or screen-reader-compatible outputs

## Why Gemma 4 Matters

- Multimodal input: understands textbook pages, handwritten notes, charts, and classroom photos
- Local-first deployment: works offline through a local runtime such as Ollama or LiteRT
- Long-context reasoning: can handle full lesson material and generate coherent plans
- Function/tool calling: can route tasks like OCR, translation, worksheet export, and quiz generation

## Competition Tracks

- Future of Education
- Digital Equity & Inclusivity
- Ollama or LiteRT Special Technology Track

## Demo Story

A teacher in a low-connectivity classroom has one textbook page and several students who learn differently. She takes one photo.

In seconds, the app creates:
- A teacher lesson plan
- A printable worksheet
- A simple-language explanation
- A local-language version
- An audio-friendly accessible explanation for a low-vision student

Later, a blind student can use the same app to study the page independently by listening to a clear explanation, asking questions, and revising with generated practice questions.

The message: one classroom photo becomes a complete inclusive lesson for teachers and an accessible study companion for students, offline.

## Winning Angle

This is not just a lesson generator and not just an accessibility tool. It is an offline inclusive classroom and study engine: the same AI helps the teacher teach and helps students, especially blind or visually impaired students, study and access the material independently.
